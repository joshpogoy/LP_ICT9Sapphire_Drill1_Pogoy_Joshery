Cryptocurrencies of the World

Bitcoin (BTC)

Bitcoin is the first decentralized cryptocurrency. Based on a free-market ideology, bitcoin was invented in 2008 when an unknown entity published a white paper under the pseudonym of Satoshi Nakamoto.

Dogecoin

Dogecoin is a cryptocurrency created by software engineers Billy Markus and Jackson Palmer, who decided to create a payment system as a joke, making fun of the wild speculation in cryptocurrencies at the time. It is considered both the first "meme coin", and more specifically the first "dog coin".

USDC

USDC is a cryptocurrency stablecoin which is issued by Circle. It is pegged to the United States dollar, and is distinct from a central bank digital currency.

XRP

XRP is the native token of Ripple, a payments network for banks and financial institutions. XRP has a total supply of 100 billion tokens, and compared to Bitcoin is less energy-intensive, offers faster transactions, and is more cost-effective.
